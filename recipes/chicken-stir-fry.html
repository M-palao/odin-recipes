Chicken Stir Fry Recipe

Description

Chicken stir-fry is a vibrant and dynamic dish known for its quick preparation and fresh flavors. It typically features tender pieces of chicken rapidly cooked alongside a colorful assortment of vegetables such as crisp broccoli, vibrant bell peppers, and crunchy carrots. The ingredients are tossed together in a hot pan or wok with a savory sauce, often a blend of soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and a touch of sweetness. The high-heat cooking method ensures the vegetables retain a pleasant bite while the chicken becomes succulent. Served piping hot, usually over fluffy rice, chicken stir-fry offers a delightful combination of textures and a balanced profile of salty, sweet, and sometimes spicy notes.

Ingridients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 cups mixed vegetables, such as broccoli florets, carrots (sliced or julienned), bell peppers (sliced), snap peas, or snow peas
  • 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil (vegetable or canola)
  • Optional: Red pepper flakes, sliced green onions, sesame seeds for garnish
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, honey or sugar, cornstarch, minced garlic, and grated ginger to create the stir-fry sauce. Set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of cooking oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the sliced chicken to the hot skillet and cook until browned and cooked through, stirring occasionally. Remove the cooked chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of cooking oil to the skillet. Add the vegetables and stir-fry for 3-5 minutes, or until they are crisp-tender.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet with the vegetables.
  6. Stir the prepared sauce again and pour it over the chicken and vegetables in the skillet.
  7. Cook, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients, about 1-2 minutes.
  8. Remove from heat. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds if desired.
  9. Serve hot over cooked rice.
